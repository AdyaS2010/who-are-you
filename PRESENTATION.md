# WHO // ARE // YOU?
### An Interactive Philosophical Exploration of Identity
*Created by Adya Sastry, Prisma Shrestha, and Breanna Barrera*

---

## 1. The Core Question
> **"Who are you at heart? What remains of the self when all external labels, memories, and social structures are stripped away?"**

This project is an investigation into the nature of personal identity. Is the self a permanent, solid core (Jackson's *qualia* / Emerson's *self-reliance*), a product of external societal frameworks (Hobbes' *Leviathan*), or a fluid, shifting bundle of perceptions with no permanent anchor (Hume's *Bundle Theory*)?

---

## 2. The Narrative Journey: Six Phases of Selfhood

* **Phase I: The Mask (Self-Description)**
  You define yourself through labels (name, traits, values, fears, aesthetic) to receive a starting Archetype. *Is this really you, or just a costume?*
* **Phase II: The World (Unconscious Profile)**
  You navigate a twilight landscape. Your actions and choices silently build a psychological alignment beneath the mask.
* **Phase III: The Shift (Context and Adaptation)**
  You are cast into a specialized environment (Strict, Chaotic, Collectivist, or Individualistic). Your identity bends under environmental pressures.
* **Phase IV: The Glitch (Dissolution)**
  The system breaks. Your labels, traits, and names dissolve in a digital void. What is left of you when your file is corrupted?
* **Phase V: The Mirror (Self-Reflection)**
  A direct reflection of your choices. Compare the self you *declared* in Phase I with the self *revealed* by your actions in Phases II-IV.
* **Phase VI: Trial of Personas (Deductive Duel)**
  An interactive "Guess Who" duel against a historical philosopher AI (Hobbes, Hume, Emerson, or Jackson). Deduce their secret theory before they solve yours.

---

## 3. Medium & Meaning: Beyond the Visuals
At first glance, a retro 2D platformer might seem like a primitive choice for conveying complex academic philosophy. But this visual simplicity is a deliberate design choice:

* **Qualia over Physicalism (Frank Jackson):** Just as Jackson's famous scientist Mary knew all physical facts about color but learned something new when she saw a red rose, you cannot understand this game merely by looking at its code or simple pixels. The value lies in the **subjective feel (qualia)** of playing, making hard choices, and experiencing the narrative.
* **Stripping the Visual Mask:** By keeping the graphics low-fidelity and retro, we strip away visual distractions. The game environment itself is a blank canvas, forcing you to focus entirely on your choices and the internal experience of who you are.

---

## 4. Game Structure & Mechanisms (Simple Outline)

The game is built purely using standard web technologies (**HTML5 Canvas** for rendering and the **Web Audio API** for interactive synthesizers).

* **The Vector Engine (Logic):** Every choice you make adjusts internal psychological dimensions (Independence vs. Conformity, Empathy, Logical Reasoning, Risk Tolerance).
* **The Coyote & Platformer Physics (Interactivity):** Smooth 2D controls, jump buffers, and "coyote time" (allowing jumps slightly after leaving a platform edge) ensure a frustrating-free, highly responsive mechanical feel.
* **The Deduction Engine (Strategy):** During the Phase VI duel, the Philosopher AI uses a mathematical split algorithm to eliminate suspects closest to a 50/50 ratio based on your answers, scaling its strategy depending on the chosen difficulty (Easy, Normal, Hard).
* **Interactive Synthesizers (Aesthetic):** Ambient sounds and sound effects are generated dynamically in real time. Different selected aesthetics (Midnight, Sunrise, Neon) scale the pitch and waveform of the background track, matching the mood of your selfhood.
